Kara Walker

06/08

Kara Walker, Born in Stockton California 1969, explores the intersection of race, gender, sexuality through her iconic silhouetted figures. Kara received her BFA from Atlanta College of Art in 1991, and MFA from Rhode Island School of Design 1994. Kara expresses that her Art explores “the racial narratives that live in our popular consciousness and culture. Where the lives of black characters are situated at the margins of white characters who exploit them.” Her works have caused a stir in the black community, some deplore her use of racist caricatures. Walker states that her intent as an artist was not to create pleasing images, nor raise questions with easy answers. “the silhouette says a lot with very little information, but that’s also what the stereotype does.”
